Vertical Articulated Robot

Robots with serial kinematics typically consisting of five or six axes that allow arbitrary movements including tilting movements in all directions.--xpertgate

------------------------------------------------------------

Vertical articulated robots are well-suited for tasks that involve working on curved surfaces or moving along circular paths. They feature a vertical column for lifting and an arm that rotates around the column, both controlled independently to easily create helical paths. This type of robot structure is similar to the human shoulder joint, and by leveraging this biomimetic design, vertical articulated robots can effortlessly perform precise and complex tasks. They are highly popular in a variety of tasks such as assembly, welding, and painting.

SCARA

Also called SCARA Robots (Selective Compliance Assembly Robot Arm) or swivel arm robots, have an articulated arm that can only swivel in the horizontal direction. The Scara robot has serial kinematics with typically four degrees of freedom, which only allows handling in plane-parallel surfaces.--xpertgate

------------------------------------------------------------

Selective Compliance Assembly Robot Arms, commonly known as SCARA robots, typically have four degrees of freedom: the base can rotate around a vertical axis, the arm can move up and down along a vertical column, and the end effector, which can rotate around its own axis, can move horizontally. This design allows for quick and precise movements confined to a single horizontal plane, making them widely popular in the assembly of small precision parts and product packaging.

Gantry Robot

Mechanical systems designed to perform tasks with a high level of precision and repeatability.--Inlogsys

------------------------------------------------------------

Gantry Robots, also known as gantry robots or Cartesian coordinate robots, typically consist of three mutually perpendicular linear motion axes (X, Y, Z), capable of achieving precise positioning and operation, playing a crucial role in scenarios that require high-precision operations. Compared to other types of robots, gantry robots can also operate in a larger working area. Therefore, engineers usually design them with higher load capacity, making them more suitable for handling large items. In addition, due to their simple structure, gantry robots also have strong flexibility and low maintenance costs, allowing them to be easily integrated into production lines. In fields such as welding, assembly, inspection, and painting, there are always the figures of gantry robots.

Cylindrical Robot

One of the most cost-effective and compact automation solutions available.--HowToRobot

------------------------------------------------------------

Cylindrical robots are mechanically simple, featuring a rotating base for rotation and two linear axes for height control and arm extension. With the addition of the appropriate end-of-arm tooling, they can be a powerful and compact robot. Due to their construction, they do not have complex movements like six-axis robots, nor do they possess the high-speed capabilities to compete with delta robots. Simple tasks that do not require high speed, payload, or joint motion, such as unloading 3D printed materials, are well-suited for cylindrical robots. Additionally, cylindrical robots are compact and have a slender mechanical structure, making them necessary for applications that require a compact solution.

Polar Robot

The Original Industrial Robot.--MWEST

------------------------------------------------------------

Polar coordinate robots have a motion system composed of three basic degrees of freedom: one rotation (θ) and two oscillations (ρ and φ). The base of these robots is fixed, and the arm rotates around a central axis, followed by further motion through one or two additional rotary joints. Their workspace is typically spherical, which gives them an advantage when dealing with tasks that require coverage in all directions. They are particularly popular in machine tool operations and assembly tasks.

Delta Robot

Very efficiently perform repetitive tasks at a highly consistent rate--MWEST

------------------------------------------------------------

Delta robots, also known as spider robots or parallel robots, are high-speed mechanical arms with three degrees of freedom, composed of three parallel arms and a central moving platform. Delta robots are renowned in the industrial field for their ability to quickly perform small, precise movements. They can handle more than 100 parts per minute, picking, placing, and sorting them at a speed much faster than what manual labor processes can execute. They are highly suitable for carrying out repetitive tasks very efficiently at a highly consistent pace. They are popular in the fields of automated boxing operations and high-precision assembly processes.

Cobot

The Robots of Choice When Working Side-by-Side with Humans--MWEST

------------------------------------------------------------

Cobots are designed to interact and work directly with humans in a shared workspace. Compared to traditional industrial robots, cobots have higher safety standards, often equipped with sensors to ensure they can slow down or stop when approaching humans, thus avoiding injury to workers. They are also easier to program, more user-friendly, and more flexible, capable of quickly adapting to changing work demands and environments. They are widely used in a variety of fields and have a significant market demand.
