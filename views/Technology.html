The Art of Robot Technology

Robotics is an interdisciplinary field involving a multitude of technologies.

Navigation and Positioning

In practical applications, robots often need to be able to move autonomously. Therefore, they must know their own location, have a map of the environment, and be able to navigate and plan routes based on this information. Navigation and positioning technologies are the keys to endowing robots with this kind of perceptual and action capability.

SLAM

SLAM is a computer vision and machine learning technology that simultaneously achieves the localization of robots and the construction of environmental maps. SLAM technology has been widely applied in many scenarios, such as autonomous driving, inspection robots, and home service robots. The core idea of SLAM technology is to observe feature points and landmarks in the environment and continuously update the robot's own position and environmental map based on its motion status. This method allows robots to act autonomously in unknown environments and build accurate maps during the process.

Path Planning Algorithms

In practical applications, robots often work in unknown or dynamically changing environments. To ensure that the robot can reach the target location and complete the task smoothly, path planning technology is integrated into the robot. This enables the robot to execute tasks more reliably and reduces the likelihood of errors and failures.

ROS

ROS is an open-source meta-operating system framework designed specifically for robot software development. It provides services similar to those of an operating system, including hardware abstraction, management of low-level drivers, execution of common functions, inter-process messaging, and package management. The main goal of ROS is to improve the software reusability in robot development and simplify the complexity and difficulty of creating complex and robust robot behaviors across different robot platforms.

MSIF

MSIF is a process that uses computer technology to automatically analyze and comprehensively process information and data from multiple sensors or sources under certain criteria. The purpose of this technology is to make the required decisions and estimates to improve the level of system intelligence. In robots, this technology enhances the reliability and robustness of the system during operation, increases the credibility of data, and improves accuracy. It also reduces the uncertainty of information, expands the system's temporal and spatial coverage, and increases the system's real-time performance and information utilization rate.
